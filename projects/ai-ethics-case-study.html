AI Ethics Case Study

Pick a real-world AI failure — bias in hiring, a recommender rabbit hole, a wrongful face-recognition arrest — reproduce it in code, and propose a fix.

What You'll Make

A 4-page report + Jupyter notebook. You'll pick a real case, build a tiny reproduction (same kind of system and data), quantify the failure, and propose a concrete mitigation you actually tested.

What You'll Need

  • Python, scikit-learn, pandas
  • Willingness to read papers and news articles critically
  • About a week of part-time effort

What You'll Learn

  • How to read an ML paper or audit report
  • How to measure group fairness (demographic parity, equalized odds)
  • Why fixes are hard — accuracy often trades off against fairness
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes This is a rich conversation space. Case studies to suggest: COMPAS recidivism, Amazon's abandoned hiring tool, Twitter's image-cropping bias, ProPublica's facial-recognition studies. Encourage reading the original papers, not just summaries.

Steps

  1. Pick a case. Recommended starters: COMPAS recidivism (ProPublica, 2016), Amazon hiring algorithm (Reuters, 2018), gender-shaded face recognition (Buolamwini, 2018).
  2. Read the original study + at least 2 critiques. Write a 1-page summary: who was affected, how was bias measured, what was the proposed fix?
  3. Reproduce a mini-version. For COMPAS, use the public dataset (github.com/propublica/compas-analysis). Train a logistic regression to predict recidivism.
  4. Measure demographic parity: is the positive rate the same across racial groups?
  5. Measure equalized odds: are the true-positive and false-positive rates equal across groups?
  6. Test a mitigation: reweight the training data, or post-process thresholds per group. Fairlearn has helpers: 
    pip install fairlearn
from fairlearn.postprocessing import ThresholdOptimizer
top = ThresholdOptimizer(estimator=model, constraints="equalized_odds")
top.fit(X, y, sensitive_features=race)
  7. Measure accuracy, false positive rate, and fairness metric before/after. Make a table.
  8. Write up your findings in 4 pages: context, method, result, and — crucially — the tradeoff. What did fairness cost?
🪄 If something goes wrong… Dataset has missing values / weird encodings? Real-world data is messy — that's part of the lesson. Document every cleaning step. Don't silently drop columns.

Try This Next

  • Interview someone affected. If safe and appropriate, include perspective from people impacted by the system you're studying.
  • Counterfactual analysis. For individual predictions, flip the sensitive attribute and compare. Should it change?
  • Policy angle. Propose one concrete policy (not technical) that would prevent the failure.
