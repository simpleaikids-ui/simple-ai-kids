AI Art Explorer

Make images with Stable Diffusion — then keep a prompt journal. Figure out which words work, which don't, and why the same prompt gives different pictures every time.

What You'll Make

A prompt journal and a gallery of ~20 generations. You'll document which keywords change the style, which break the model, and share your three favorites.

What You'll Need

  • Google Colab (free GPU)
  • A Hugging Face account (free)
  • About 90 minutes, then as long as you want

What You'll Learn

  • How text-to-image models turn text into pixels (roughly)
  • What "seed" means and why it matters for reproducibility
  • The ethics of AI art — training data and artist credit
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes Stable Diffusion models can occasionally generate unexpected NSFW output. Stick to SFW-finetuned models (e.g. "stable-diffusion-2-1-base" or newer). Discuss the training-data ethics conversation — models learned from millions of artists' work, often without consent.

Steps

  1. Create a free account at huggingface.co and accept the Stable Diffusion model license.
  2. Open Colab → Runtime → Change runtime type → GPU (T4 is free).
  3. Install: 
    !pip install -q diffusers transformers accelerate
  4. Load the model: 
    import torch
from diffusers import StableDiffusionPipeline
pipe = StableDiffusionPipeline.from_pretrained("stabilityai/stable-diffusion-2-1-base", torch_dtype=torch.float16)
pipe.to("cuda")
  5. Your first image: 
    img = pipe("a red panda astronaut eating ramen in space, digital art").images[0]
img
  6. Prompt journal: open a text doc. Run 10 prompts. For each, save the prompt, the seed, and paste the image.
  7. Experiment: keep the subject, change the style. "…, oil painting", "…, pixel art", "…, photorealistic", "…, in the style of Studio Ghibli." What shifts?
  8. Lock the seed to get reproducibility: 
    g = torch.Generator("cuda").manual_seed(42)
img = pipe("a red panda astronaut", generator=g).images[0]
  9. Push the limits: try prompts about hands, text in the image, or "photo of a 1980s album cover." Where does it fail?
  10. Write a one-page reflection: who should get credit for an AI image? Why?
🪄 If something goes wrong… Out of memory? Use pipe.enable_attention_slicing(). Images look blurry? Try 30–50 inference steps: pipe(prompt, num_inference_steps=40). Model won't load? Make sure you accepted the license on Hugging Face.

Try This Next

  • Image-to-image. Start from a rough sketch and let the model finish it with StableDiffusionImg2ImgPipeline.
  • ControlNet. Use a pose or edge map as input. Much more controllable than prompts alone.
  • Compare models. Try SDXL, Flux, or DALL·E 3 via API. Same prompt — who wins?
