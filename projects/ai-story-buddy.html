AI Story Buddy

Pick a hero, a place, and a problem — then ask an AI to write you a brand-new story. Become the illustrator: draw the pictures for the best scenes!

What You'll Make

A one-of-a-kind storybook. You invent the ingredients (a hero, a setting, a problem), the AI writes the story, and you illustrate at least three scenes. Staple it together for a real book!

What You'll Need

  • A computer with a chatbot (your grown-up opens it)
  • Paper or a small notebook
  • Crayons, markers, or colored pencils
  • A stapler, if you want to make a real book
  • About 30 minutes

What You'll Learn

  • That AI makes stories from the ingredients you choose
  • That the same ingredients can make very different stories
  • That you are the boss of the story — the AI is just helping
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes You type the prompt on behalf of the child. Pre-check the output for anything unexpected (violence, adult themes) before reading aloud — chatbots occasionally go off the rails even with a kid-friendly prompt. If something feels off, ask the bot to "rewrite for a 6-year-old, no scary parts."

Steps

  1. Pick your hero. A bunny? A pirate? You? Write it down on paper.
  2. Pick your setting. The moon? A candy forest? Grandma's kitchen? Write it down.
  3. Pick the problem. Hero is lost? Hungry? Stuck in a tree? Write it down.
  4. Now ask your grown-up to type this prompt into the chatbot: "Write a short, funny story for a 6-year-old. The hero is a [YOUR HERO]. It's set in a [YOUR SETTING]. The problem is [YOUR PROBLEM]. Make it about 5 paragraphs long."
  5. Read the story out loud together. Laugh at the funny parts.
  6. Pick three scenes from the story you want to illustrate. Fold paper in half or quarters — one drawing per page.
  7. Draw each scene. Add the hero's face. Color the background.
  8. On the last page, write: "The End — story by [YOUR NAME] and an AI friend." Staple it together!
🪄 If something goes wrong… Is the story boring or confusing? Ask the grown-up to type "Try again — make it funnier and sillier" or "Shorter, more like a picture book." Giving feedback is how you get better stories.

Try This Next

  • Remix it. Keep the hero and setting. Change ONLY the problem. Is the new story totally different?
  • Ask for a different ending. Tell the AI: "Rewrite the last paragraph so the hero finds a dragon friend."
  • Write a sequel. Ask for story #2 with the same hero and setting but a new problem.
