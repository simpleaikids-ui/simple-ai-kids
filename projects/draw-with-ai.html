Draw with AI

Scribble on the screen and watch a computer try to guess what you're drawing — and see how real artists turn simple shapes into amazing art.

What You'll Make

You'll play Quick, Draw! — a free game by Google where you have 20 seconds to draw something like a cat or a pizza, and the computer tries to guess what it is. The computer gets smarter every time anyone plays!

How it works

What You'll Need

  • A computer or tablet with a browser
  • A mouse, trackpad, or touch screen (drawing works on all of them)
  • About 10 minutes

What You'll Learn

  • That computers can learn from millions of drawings by other kids
  • Why drawings that look the same to you can confuse a computer
  • What "training data" means — it's just examples!
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes Quick, Draw! is free and browser-based. No account needed. Google uses the drawings (anonymously) as training data for their public doodle dataset. No personal info is collected with a drawing. Works great on Chromebooks and iPads.

Steps

  1. Open quickdraw.withgoogle.com in your browser.
  2. Click the big yellow Let's Draw! button.
  3. The computer will tell you what to draw — like "draw a banana." You have 20 seconds. Go!
  4. As you draw, listen for the computer's guesses. It says them out loud: "I see a circle… oh, a banana!"
  5. When the round ends, the computer shows you six drawings. You did five of them. Try to finish all six!
  6. At the end, click on your drawings. See how other kids drew the same thing. Some look totally different!
  7. Click on one of the things the computer didn't guess. Why couldn't it tell? Was your drawing too tiny? Too squiggly? That's called being hard to classify.
🪄 If something goes wrong… Did the computer never guess your drawing? Try making it bigger — fill up more of the box. Also try drawing the main part first. If you draw the eyes before the face, the computer gets confused.

Try This Next

  • Draw it wrong on purpose. Try to draw a cat that looks like a dog. Can you trick the computer?
  • Play a whole game with a grown-up. Take turns. Who gets more correct guesses?
  • Look at the giant dataset. Visit quickdraw.withgoogle.com/data and click any category. You'll see thousands of drawings from kids all over the world. Notice how different they all look!
