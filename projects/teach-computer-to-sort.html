Teach the Computer to Sort

Train a webcam to tell apart two things — like a banana and an apple, or a happy face and a silly face. Feels like magic!

What You'll Make

You'll show the computer two kinds of things, like an apple and a banana. After a few tries, the computer will guess which one you're holding up — all by itself!

How the Computer Learns — Pictures → Brain → Guess

What You'll Need

  • A computer with a webcam
  • An adult to help open the webpage
  • Two different things to show the camera (fruit, toys, stuffed animals)
  • About 15 minutes

What You'll Learn

  • How computers learn from examples — just like you do
  • Why more examples make the computer smarter
  • That you can teach the computer to recognize anything
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes This project uses Google's Teachable Machine. No account is needed. Training happens in the browser — webcam images never leave your computer unless you choose to save the model. It works on any Chromebook, Mac, or Windows laptop with a webcam and a modern browser. Kindergartners and 1st graders will need help clicking buttons and reading prompts; by age 7 most kids can drive it themselves.

Steps

  1. Ask your grown-up to open teachablemachine.withgoogle.com in a browser. Click the big blue button that says Get Started.
  2. Click Image Project, then pick Standard image model.
  3. You'll see two boxes called Class 1 and Class 2. Click the words Class 1 and type what's in it, like Apple.
  4. Pick up your first thing (the apple) and hold it in front of the webcam. Click and hold the Webcam button under Class 1. Move the apple around — spin it, tilt it, move it closer and farther — so the computer sees it from lots of angles. Take about 30 photos.
  5. Now rename Class 2 (like Banana) and do the same thing for your second object.
  6. Click the Train Model button. Wait a few seconds — the computer is studying the pictures!
  7. When it's done, hold up your apple in front of the camera. Watch the bar on the right! Then try the banana. Then try showing both! What happens if you show something new, like your hand?
🪄 If something goes wrong… If the computer guesses wrong, you probably need more examples. Go back to the class that's confusing it and take more photos — especially at different angles and distances.

Try This Next

  • Make it harder. Try three classes instead of two — apple, banana, and orange!
  • Face game. Make classes for "happy face," "sad face," and "silly face." See if the computer can read your feelings.
  • Trick it. Try showing the computer something totally different. Does it still pick one? That's called overconfidence — computers don't always know when they don't know!
