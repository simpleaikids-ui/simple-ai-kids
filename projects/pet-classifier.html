Pet Classifier

Teach a computer to tell your pet apart from other animals — and discover why fair training data matters.

What You'll Make

A pet recognition app. Train the computer on your pet (or a stuffed animal), plus two other animal classes. Then test it on pictures from the internet. Does it work? What goes wrong?

What You'll Need

  • A computer with a webcam, or a folder of 30+ photos of your pet
  • Phone to take reference pictures
  • Machine Learning for Kids or Teachable Machine
  • About 45 minutes

What You'll Learn

  • Why a model trained on one dog may fail on another
  • What representation means in training data
  • How to test a model honestly by holding out test data
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes This project has a built-in ethics lesson. Pre-download 5 test photos from the internet (search "golden retriever" on an image site) for the kid to evaluate with after training. That "surprise" test is the whole point.

Steps

  1. Pick your pet. Dog, cat, guinea pig, stuffed animal — anything works.
  2. Set up three classes in Teachable Machine: my-pet, other-animal, nothing.
  3. Take 40 webcam photos of your pet. Get all angles — face, side, sleeping, playing.
  4. For other-animal, take 40 photos of a totally different animal (a sibling pretending to be a cat works, or print/find pictures).
  5. For nothing, take 30 photos of the empty room or background objects.
  6. Train the model. It should be ~95% confident on your pet.
  7. Now the honest test: your grown-up downloads 5 internet photos of the same species as your pet (different dogs if yours is a dog). Test each one.
  8. Write down results: how many did it correctly call my-pet? How many said other-animal? Why?
  9. Fix attempt: add 10 photos of different dogs to the other-animal class. Retrain. Better?
🪄 If something goes wrong… Model calls all dogs "my-pet"? Your pet images probably showed the same room and angle every time. Retake photos in different places. Better yet, use 10 real photos of other dogs in the "other-animal" class.

Try This Next

  • Ethics diary. Write 3 ways a poorly-trained model could cause problems if it were used in the real world.
  • Siblings test. Does the model recognize your sibling's dog? If so, what's really being learned?
  • Breed hunt. Add 3 classes for 3 dog breeds. How does accuracy change?
