Emoji Detective

Teach the computer to recognize your thumbs-up, thumbs-down, and silliest face — then play Emoji Detective with a friend!

What You'll Make

A webcam-powered game. You train the computer on three poses (👍 👎 🤪), then you and a friend take turns showing poses — the computer calls out what you did!

What You'll Need

  • A computer with a webcam
  • A grown-up to help at the start
  • About 20 minutes
  • A friend to play with at the end (optional but fun!)

What You'll Learn

  • That AI gets smarter with more examples
  • Why showing poses from different angles matters
  • What happens when you don't give the model a "nothing" option
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes Uses Google's Teachable Machine (browser-based, images never leave the device). Make sure the webcam is working before you start. Good lighting helps a LOT — a bright lamp in front of the child works better than overhead light.

Steps

  1. Open teachablemachine.withgoogle.com. Click Get Started → Image Project → Standard image model.
  2. You'll see two class boxes. Click + Add a class so you have three total.
  3. Rename them: thumbs-up, thumbs-down, silly face.
  4. Click Webcam under thumbs-up. Hold up 👍 and press and hold the record button. Move your thumb — close, far, left, right — take about 30 pictures.
  5. Do the same for thumbs-down (👎).
  6. For silly face, stick out your tongue, cross your eyes, make your silliest face. 30 pictures.
  7. Click the big Train Model button. Wait 10–20 seconds.
  8. Once it's trained, show the camera a thumbs-up! The bar should jump to thumbs-up. Try all three.
  9. Play Emoji Detective: close your eyes and have your friend make a pose behind you. Open eyes, look at the camera guess, then turn around to check. Did the computer call it right?
🪄 If something goes wrong… Does it always pick the same class? Take more examples for the class it's ignoring. Does it get confused between two classes? Make them look more different — bigger motions, different distances.

Try This Next

  • Add a "nothing" class. Take 30 photos of just the empty room. Does the model get better?
  • Test with different lighting. Turn on a different lamp. Does it still work? Why or why not?
  • Invite different people. Does your model work on your sibling's thumbs-up? Why might it fail?

🎯 Quick Quiz

What's the best way to make your model smarter?

Why might the model get your sibling's thumbs-up wrong?
