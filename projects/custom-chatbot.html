Build Your Own Chatbot

Teach a Scratch chatbot to recognize three kinds of messages — greetings, questions, and goodbyes — and reply the right way every time.

What You'll Make

A chatbot that knows when you're saying hello, asking a question, or saying goodbye. You'll train it on your own examples, then hook it up to Scratch so it can chat back.

How the Bot Picks an Answer

What You'll Need

  • A computer with a browser (Chromebook works great)
  • A free Machine Learning for Kids account (a grown-up helps set this up)
  • About 45 minutes
  • A notebook to plan your chatbot's replies

What You'll Learn

  • What "training data" means and why more is better
  • How a computer can understand what kind of message you're typing
  • How to connect a model to a game in Scratch
🧑‍🍳 Grown-Up Notes This uses Machine Learning for Kids by Dale Lane, a free, kid-focused tool that pairs with Scratch. An adult needs to create a teacher/student account (it's free), and students get a class code. Data and examples stay within ML4Kids. You'll find a short teacher's guide on their site. Budget an extra 10 minutes for account setup if you haven't used it before.

Plan It First (No Computer Needed)

Before you touch the computer, grab a notebook and plan these three things:

  1. Write down 5 different ways someone might say hello. (Hi! Hey there. What's up? Howdy! Good morning.)
  2. Write down 5 different questions someone might ask. (What's your name? How old are you? What's your favorite color?)
  3. Write down 5 different ways someone might say goodbye. (Bye! See ya. Talk later. Gotta go. Peace!)

Build It

  1. Log in to Machine Learning for Kids and click Go to your projects → + Add a new project.
  2. Name it My Chatbot. Pick Text as the type of learning. Click Create.
  3. Click your project → Train. Add three labels: greeting, question, goodbye.
  4. Type your 5 examples for each label into the correct box. (This is the training data!) The more examples, the smarter your bot.
  5. Click the Learn & Test button, then Train new machine learning model. Wait about a minute.
  6. Once it's trained, type a new sentence into the test box. Your model will guess which label fits. Try to stump it!
  7. Now click Make → Scratch 3. A Scratch window opens with new blocks specific to your model.
  8. Drag this script onto your sprite (the cat): when green flag clicked → forever → ask "Say something!" → if recognise text (answer) as greeting → say "Hi friend!"
  9. Add two more branches for question ("Hmm, let me think…") and goodbye ("Catch you later!").
  10. Click the green flag and talk to your chatbot!
🪄 If something goes wrong… Does the bot always pick the same label? You probably need more variety in your examples. Add 5 more to the label that's being ignored. Does it get confused by short messages? Add some short examples to your training data.

Try This Next

  • Add a new kind of message. Try adding a compliment label. What happens when you say "You're cool"?
  • Make your bot moody. Have it reply differently on different days using Scratch's pick random block.
  • Ethics moment: Train the bot on only formal greetings. Does it get confused when someone says "sup"? That's a kind of bias — the bot only knows what it's seen.
