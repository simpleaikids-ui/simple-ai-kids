Staying Safe with AI

A quick guide for kids — and a reminder for grown-ups — about keeping AI projects fun and safe.

Rules for Kids

  • Don't tell a chatbot your full name, address, school, or phone number.
  • Don't upload photos of friends, classmates, or family without asking them first.
  • If a chatbot says something mean, weird, or scary, stop and tell a grown-up.
  • Don't believe everything a chatbot tells you — it can be wrong, even when it sounds sure.
  • Never download a file a chatbot tells you to download without checking with a grown-up.

Rules for Grown-Ups

  • Sit near your child the first time they use a chatbot so you can talk about what shows up.
  • Use age-appropriate AI tools. Most "general" chatbots require users to be 13+.
  • Check the privacy policy of any tool before linking a paid account or real data.
  • Discuss bias openly. AI makes mistakes — those are teaching moments.
  • For API-based projects, store keys in environment files, never in code they'll share.

If Something Feels Wrong

Close the tab. Tell an adult. You can always come back later. No AI project is worth continuing if something makes you uncomfortable.
